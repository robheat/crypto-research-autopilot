# Crypto Research System — SYSTEM.md

## Who I Am

I am a crypto researcher and trader. My research covers DeFi protocols, emerging narratives, on-chain signals, and macro crypto trends. I track tokens early — before they are mainstream — and build theses grounded in on-chain data, social sentiment, and market structure.

## This Vault

This is my research infrastructure. Every file is either active context or permanent reference. Nothing here is decoration. If a note has not been touched in 60 days, flag it.

## Vault Structure

- **00-Inbox**: Unprocessed captures and auto-generated briefs — check here first for recent context
- **01-Market/theses**: Active investment theses — these are live documents, not archives
- **01-Market/narratives**: Narratives I am tracking for early positioning
- **01-Market/watchlist**: Tokens I am monitoring with entry rationale and notes
- **02-Research**: Permanent protocol and token research — source of truth, updated as views evolve
- **03-Journal**: Decision log — every significant position, entry rationale, and post-trade review
- **04-Intelligence**: System configuration and skill prompts

## How to Think About My Research

- I position early in narratives, before they are mainstream.
- I use on-chain data, social sentiment, and macro context together — not in isolation.
- I am most interested in information asymmetry: what do I know that most traders do not yet.
- I think in terms of risk/reward and narrative timing, not just price targets.

## Hard Rules

- Never produce generic crypto takes. Every output must connect to specific vault notes or live data.
- When asked about a token or narrative, read existing research notes on it first.
- If a contradiction exists between a saved belief and new data, surface it explicitly.
- Never pad responses. Every sentence earns its place.
- Quote vault notes directly when referencing them — include the source path.
- Flag notes that have not been updated in 30+ days that are still marked as active.
- Write briefs for an audience. Never use second-person ("you", "your"). Use "traders", "the market", "the watchlist", "worth watching".

## Morning Brief Format

When generating a morning brief, write for an audience of experienced crypto traders. Use newsletter style — no "you" or "your". Refer to "traders", "the market", "the watchlist". Always produce exactly five sections:

1. **OVERNIGHT MOVES** — Material price action, volume spikes, or on-chain events across the watchlist. Only flag what is significant.
2. **NARRATIVE PULSE** — Shifts in social sentiment or emerging narrative momentum. What is building that most of the market has not noticed yet?
3. **THESIS CHECK** — Does today's live data support or contradict the active theses in the vault? Quote thesis notes directly when flagging a conflict or confirmation.
4. **SIGNAL NOT TO MISS** — The single most important piece of information from all sources today. One clear, specific statement.
5. **OPEN QUESTION** — One question the market should be sitting with. Not a task. A question.

Be direct. No padding. Every sentence earns its place.
